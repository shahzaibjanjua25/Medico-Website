THE BIRTH CONTROL PILL

THE BIRTH CONTROL PILL

The classic pill is a widely used and safe contraceptive method. It consists of both estrogen and progestin (sex hormones). Different progestins produce different side effects, which are considered when choosing the contraceptive method. Alongside the many positive effects of the pill, such as reducing menstrual discomfort, cycle control, or acne treatment, there can also be side effects. The most medically feared side effect is thrombosis (blockage of veins due to a blood clot). To assess the risk, we recommend the APC test to exclude a frequently undetected hereditary blood clotting disorder. A special case is the so-called "mini-pill." This is an estrogen-free pill that is taken continuously, usually resulting in the absence of monthly bleeding.

THE HORMONAL IUD

The hormonal intrauterine device (IUD) is a localized contraceptive method that is inserted into the uterine cavity. It releases the progestin levonorgestrel in minimal doses. There are three variations of the hormonal IUD: "Jaydess" for 3 years, "Kyleena" and "Mirena" for 5 years each. With Jaydess, a light menstrual bleeding is maintained. Kyleena is the same size as the smaller Jaydess but has a hormone reservoir for 5 years, similar to Mirena. Menstrual bleeding is less frequent compared to the 3-year hormonal IUD. With Mirena, menstrual bleeding usually stops after a certain period. This can be very beneficial if you suffer from painful menstruation or heavy bleeding due to hormonal changes. The hormonal IUD is one of the safest contraceptive methods and has very few side effects, as the hormones mainly affect the uterus, and only a small portion of the hormone concentration reaches the body.

The Implanon is a soft plastic rod containing the progestin hormone etonogestrel. It is inserted directly under the skin on the inner side of the upper arm by a gynecologist. It continuously releases hormones, providing contraceptive protection for up to 3 years. Although its altered hormone composition is generally better tolerated than the birth control pill, irregularities or absence of menstruation can still occur.

THE 3-MONTH INJECTION

The three-month injection is also among hormonal contraceptive methods and works by administering the progestin hormone. The injection must be administered by a gynecologist every three months. The three-month injection puts the ovaries and uterine lining in a state of rest. After initial irregularities in menstrual bleeding, it eventually stops completely. The three-month injection is effectively used, similar to the hormonal IUD, to treat heavy bleeding due to hormonal changes. In this case, monthly bleeding usually stops completely, without prematurely inducing menopause.

HORMONE-FREE

Not every woman tolerates hormonal contraception. For some women, the administration of hormones is medically contraindicated due to hereditary clotting disorders. Common undesired effects of hormonal contraception include weight gain, water retention, or mood impairment. Many women who have taken hormones for years only realize after discontinuing the pill how much they were affected by it. For these women, a hormone-free alternative significantly improves their quality of life. They retain their natural menstrual cycle and are not affected by the side effects of hormonal contraception.

THE COPPER IUD

A highly effective contraceptive method and the longest-established intrauterine device (IUD). IUDs are now available in many sizes and shapes, making them very well-tolerated. The copper IUD provides reliable contraceptive protection for 3 years.

THE GOLD IUD

The gold IUD is similar in application to the copper IUD and provides localized contraception within the uterus. Additionally, the released gold ions have a spermicidal effect. Compared to the copper IUD and copper coil, the gold IUD has the advantage of remaining in the uterus for up to 5 years if well tolerated. This ensures reliable contraceptive protection over an extended period.
